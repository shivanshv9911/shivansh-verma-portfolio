M.Tech – Computational Biology• August 2021-August 2023

CGPA : 9.0/10

Thesis Title: Analysis of Gene Expression and Structural Variations in Common Genes Across Populations: Implications for Cardiovascular Disease and Reverse Cholesterol Pathway

Description: This project investigates how genetic variations influence lipid metabolism and contribute to cardiovascular disease (CVD). By integrating data from three genomic studies, we identified key genes affecting lipid balance. Using AutoDock Vina for molecular docking and Kolmogorov–Smirnov (K-S) tests for statistical analysis, we screened 54,162 compounds to assess changes in protein-ligand binding energies caused by genetic variants.

Key findings: Identified significant variants (e.g., in NPC1, ABCA1, and THOC5) that alter binding affinities and potentially disrupt lipid metabolism. These results provide molecular-level insights into how genetics affect cholesterol balance and the Reverse Cholesterol Transport pathway, with implications for CVD treatment.

Tools Used:
  • Molecular Docking: AutoDock Vina
  • Statistical Analysis: Kolmogorov–Smirnov (K-S) test
  • Compound Library: 54,162 compounds for virtual screening
